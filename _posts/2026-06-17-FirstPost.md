My Journey Toward Becoming an LLM Engineer (2026–2028)
Over the next three years, I’m committing myself to a clear and ambitious goal:
becoming a Large Language Model Engineer capable of contributing to cutting‑edge AI research and production systems — and ultimately joining a company like Mistral AI.

This blog will document that journey: the skills I’m building, the projects I’m shipping, the papers I’m studying, and the lessons I learn along the way.

Why I’m Doing This
I’m fascinated by the intersection of deep learning, systems engineering, and large‑scale model optimization. LLMs are not just models — they are systems: distributed training pipelines, inference engines, retrieval architectures, and complex software stacks that require both mathematical understanding and engineering discipline.

My goal is to become the kind of engineer who can:

train models

optimize them

deploy them

evaluate them

and understand them deeply

This blog is my way of staying accountable and sharing what I learn.

My Roadmap (2026–2028)
I’ve built a structured, multi‑year plan to reach an LLM Engineer level.
Here is the high‑level overview.

1. Core Engineering Skills (2026–2027)
Before touching advanced LLM systems, I need a rock‑solid engineering foundation.

Python (Engineer‑level)
Typing, clean logging, modular scripts, error handling, async/multiprocessing, and unit testing with pytest.

Machine Learning Foundations
Regression, SVMs, Random Forests, XGBoost, cross‑validation, overfitting, and metrics like F1, ROC, and perplexity.

Deep Learning with PyTorch
Tensors, autograd, optimizers, training loops from scratch, CNN/RNN/MLP, and regularization.

These fundamentals will support everything that comes next.

2. LLM‑Specific Skills (the core of my path)
This is the heart of my roadmap.

Transformers
Attention, multi‑head attention, RoPE, LayerNorm, residuals, KV cache, FlashAttention concepts.

Fine‑Tuning
SFT, LoRA, QLoRA, PEFT, dataset structuring, and before/after evaluation.

RAG (Retrieval‑Augmented Generation)
Chunking, embeddings, vector DBs (FAISS, Milvus), reranking, multi‑source RAG, and evaluation.

Inference Optimization
vLLM, quantization (GPTQ, AWQ), dynamic batching, optimized KV cache, streaming, latency vs throughput.

Distributed Training
DDP, FSDP, DeepSpeed, sharding, mixed precision (FP16/BF16), GPU profiling.

This is the skillset required to work on real LLM systems.

3. Systems & DevOps (required for production‑grade AI)
Infrastructure
Docker, FastAPI, Kubernetes basics, monitoring (Prometheus/Grafana), and cloud (AWS/GCP).

Software Architecture
Modular projects, clean patterns, config management (Hydra/YAML), reproducible pipelines.

LLM engineering is as much about systems as it is about models.
